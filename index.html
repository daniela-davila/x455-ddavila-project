TOMATO SALAD

Savor the essence of summer with this enticing tomato salad recipe. Crafted with the freshest ingredients, including juicy tomatoes, thinly sliced red onions, and fragrant basil leaves.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe tomatoes, preferably heirloom or vine-ripened
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, torn into small pieces
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. Wash the tomatoes and basil leaves thoroughly under cold water. Pat them dry with a paper towel.
  2. Slice the tomatoes into medium-thick slices and arrange them on a serving plate or in a bowl.
  3. Scatter the torn basil leaves over the sliced tomatoes.
  4. Drizzle the extra virgin olive oil and balsamic vinegar over the tomatoes and basil. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Gently toss the salad to ensure all ingredients are evenly coated with the dressing.
  6. Let the salad sit for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Serve and enjoy your refreshing tomato and basil salad!

You can adjust the quantities of olive oil, balsamic vinegar, salt, and pepper according to your taste preferences. Additionally, feel free to add other ingredients like mozzarella cheese, red onion slices, or olives for extra flavor and texture.

GREEK SALAD

This greek salad is best enjoyed fresh, but you can prepare the ingredients ahead of time and toss them together with the dressing just before serving to keep everything crisp and flavorful. Enjoy the vibrant flavors of Greece in every bite!

Ingredients:

  • 4 medium tomatoes, cut into wedges
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper (red, yellow, or green), seeded and sliced
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives, pitted
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Optional: 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley or mint for garnish
  1. In a large salad bowl, combine the tomato wedges, sliced cucumber, red onion, bell pepper slices, and Kalamata olives.
  2. In a small bowl or jar, whisk together the extra virgin olive oil, red wine vinegar, dried oregano, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad ingredients in the bowl and toss gently to coat everything evenly.
  4. Sprinkle the crumbled feta cheese over the top of the salad.
  5. If using, garnish the salad with chopped fresh parsley or mint.

Serve immediately as a refreshing side dish or as a light meal on its own.

OATMEAL PECAN COOKIES

Enjoy these delicious oatmeal pecan cookies with a glass of milk or your favorite hot beverage! They're perfect for a sweet treat any time of day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1/2 cup chopped pecans

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or silicone baking mat.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until light and fluffy, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Beat in the egg and vanilla extract until well combined.
  5. Gradually add the flour mixture to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  6. Stir in the rolled oats and chopped pecans until evenly distributed throughout the dough.
  7. Using a spoon or cookie scoop, drop rounded tablespoonfuls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  8. Flatten each cookie slightly with the back of a spoon or your fingertips.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown.

Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cooled, store the oatmeal pecan cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to one week.
